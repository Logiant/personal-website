Mechatronic Crane

A mechatronic crane system built over the fall semester of 2015. The entire system was fabricated, wired, and programmed within the span of a couple months. Powered by an Arduino Mega 2650 running Simulink, the system positions an end effector in real time based on user input from a custom Matlab GUI.

Mass Transport Via Diffusion

To determine the useful lifespan of an Expo dry erase marker an analytical model of the marker was developed and compared to experimental data. Using a custom built whiteboard turntable the marker drew for six hours and was weighed intermittently. The results? Dry erase markers are good for about three hours of constant writing.

Pneumatic Walking Robot

As a capstone research project at the Milwaukee School of Engineering a pneumatic walking robot was designed and built. It included a sophisticated control system running on an Arduino Mega 2650 powered by onboard battery and pneumatic valve manifold. The entire system was controlled wirelessly using an Xbox 360 controller running through a custom Java GUI. In addition a full kinematic and dynamic model of the robot were created and used to support the design.
